Block 1 · Context↑ Back to the player

Use case

In-house legal compares each contract (NDA, MSA, DPA) against a template, flags deviations and proposes wording, keeping the negotiation traceable. Today: manual reading with track-changes and precedent-hunting in email.

Block 2 · Solution↑ Back to the player

How to solve it

Ollama + Llama 3.1 70B over PDF + template. Prompt: compare clause by clause, flag material deviations, don't invent case law.

Block 4 · Risk↑ Back to the player

Vulnerabilities & risks

  • "If I upload the MSA to a public service, I lose attorney-client privilege."
  • "If the model hallucinates case law and it is cited, the firm is sanctioned."
  • "contracts:write scope → the agent signs with no human gate."
  • "The agent runs with a shared service credential: I cannot tell which lawyer asked what, and I cannot revoke per case."
  • "The citation validator and the drafter are the same process: no identity separation, no auditable A2A."

Shadow AI

"An unregistered MCP nobody inventoried runs with no identity or control."

Covered by the governance in the remediation architecture (block 5). Regulation: legal privilege, GDPR/AEPD, EU AI Act.
Block 5 · Remediation↑ Back to the player

Remediation

The agent runs on a shared service credential, talks directly to a public LLM and to MCPs with no allowlist. No per-lawyer identity, no per-matter trace, no human gate on contracts:write.
